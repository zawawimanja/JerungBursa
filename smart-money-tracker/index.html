📚 Trading SOP & Guide Board

1. Why Consolidation Bases Form?

It represents the price zone where Smart Money is passively accumulating shares near their **52-Week High / ATH** (pullback ≤ 15.0%) without driving the price up. The lower limit (Support Floor) acts as a strong defense line.

2. Entry Strategy (Buy Zone)

Enter the market when the current price is extremely close to the Support Floor (distance ≤ 2.5%) and in a healthy pullback from its 52W peak (pullback ≤ 15.0%). This provides a low-risk entry and optimal risk-reward ratio.

3. Risk Management

Stop Loss (SL) is placed strictly 3% below the Support Floor. If the price breaks below this floor, it signifies the accumulation structure has failed, and we exit with a minimal loss.

4. Take Profit (TP) Execution

TP1 is set at +10.0% from entry for quick swing profits. TP2 is set at +20.0% to capture the full ATH breakout / RBS momentum.

5. Strict SOP vs. Standard SOP

👑 Strict SOP (OGX, HKB, KEEMING Style): Must be near the **All-Time High (ATH) or 52-Week High (Pullback ≤ 15.0%)** + Must have a Lower Wick Rejection (Reject Ekor Bawah 🧬) on the **Daily (1D) Timeframe** proving that Smart Money is actively buying at the floor. This setup yields the highest win-rate and fastest breakout speed.
⚠️ Standard SOP: Filters all counters consolidating close to their support floor (≤ 3.0%) and near peaks (pullback ≤ 15.0%), but without a prominent daily wick rejection today.

6. Dual-Window Floor (Staircase Breakouts & IPOs)

To accurately capture high-momentum stocks forming staircase breakouts (higher base consolidations like TMK) or recent IPOs with short history (< 25 trading days like MMCS), the engine dynamically adapts between 10-day and 5-day support floors. If the 10-day floor is too far (> 3.0%) but a new 5-day base is established (≤ 3.0%) with strong touch validation (TouchCount ≥ 3x harian), the system automatically triggers an entry signal.
